First Trimester

The first trimester is from conception to 12 weeks.

There are a variety of common symptoms that can appear in the first trimester including:

  • Morning sickness
  • Fatigue
  • Swollen breasts
  • Frequent urination
  • Food aversions or cravings
  • Changes in bowel movements

Don't worry, fatigue and morning sickness typically subside in the second trimester.

Things you may want to think about or accomplish in your first trimester:

  • Start taking a prenatal vitamin ASAP
  • See your doctor
  • Review safety for pregnancy (foods, activities, etc)
  • Check your health insurance
  • Quit smoking and drinking
  • Limit caffeine and drink plenty of water
  • Exercise 30 minutes daily
  • Eat healthy foods

You may be able to find out the sex of your baby in the first trimester through a blood test called "NIPT" or "Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing." Though it can be confirmed via ultrasound in the first trimester, it is likely too early to accurately assess, so it will be confirmed in your second trimester.

Second Trimester

The second trimester is from week 13 to week 27.

There are a variety of common symptoms that can appear in the first trimester including:

  • Growing belly and breasts
  • Skin changes
  • Nasal problems
  • Frequent urination
  • Increased appetite
  • Braxton Hicks contractions
  • Leg cramps
  • Bleeding gums

You will likely begin to show your baby bump. Baby's kicks will make their appearance between 18 and 24 weeks, only to get stronger and more frequent thereafter.

Things you may want to think about or accomplish in your second trimester:

  • Creating a registry
  • Baby shower invites sent out
  • Booking maternity photos
  • Getting on waitlists for daycare
  • Getting the nursery together (while you still have a boost in energy)
  • Shopping for maternity clothing
  • Begin interviewing pediatricians
  • See your dentist

As always, make sure you're keeping up your healthy habits.

Third Trimester

The third trimester is from week 28 to week 40.

Home stretch! How exciting?! These are some symptoms you may experience:

  • Growing belly and breasts
  • Backaches
  • Heartburn
  • Frequent urination
  • Shortness of breath
  • Braxton Hicks contractions
  • Spider veins, varicose veins, and hemorrhoids

Here are some things you should consider before baby makes their way into the world:

  • Do your kick counts
  • Enjoy your baby shower
  • Take classes: childbirth, carring for your newborn, etc.
  • Create a birthplan
  • Pack your hospital bag
  • Learn the stages of labor and signs you may need to see your doctor
  • Finalize the nursery
  • Narrow down baby names
  • Talk to your baby

Congrats! You made it!
